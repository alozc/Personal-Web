About

About

I am a senior at Arizona State University studying Finance, Business Data Analytics, and Supply Chain Management. These disciplines may seem different, but to me they are connected by one main question: how does a company create value, and where can it improve?

My goal is to look at business problems from different perspectives and find ways to make companies more efficient, profitable, and prepared for growth. I am interested in understanding not only the numbers, but also the operations and decisions behind those numbers.

I enjoy work that connects financial analysis, operational improvement, and data-driven insights. For example, a logistics inefficiency can become a margin problem, and a financial metric may only make sense when the operational reality behind it is understood. That connection is what interests me most.

I am especially interested in work where I can analyze a company, understand how it operates, and use data to identify opportunities for improvement and investment into new markets for expansion. I also want to continue learning about business growth, emerging markets, and how capital can be used to create meaningful and long-term value.

Currently exploring

  • How operational improvements — not just financial leverage — drive real returns
  • Supply chain analytics: spotting inefficiencies that compound into margin
  • How capital moves through different asset classes and emerging markets
  • Financial modeling and valuation under different scenarios and structures
  • The intersection of data analytics, finance, and operations strategy
